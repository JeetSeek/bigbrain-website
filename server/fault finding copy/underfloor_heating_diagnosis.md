# Underfloor Heating Fault Finding Guide

This comprehensive guide addresses diagnostic procedures for both water-based and electric underfloor heating systems, covering common faults, testing methods, and remedial actions.

## PART 1: UNDERSTANDING UNDERFLOOR HEATING SYSTEMS

### Types of Underfloor Heating Systems

**1. Water-Based (Wet) Systems**
- Heated water circulates through pipes beneath the floor
- Connected to boiler or heat pump
- Typically uses manifold to distribute water to zones
- Controlled via thermostats, actuators, and pumps
- Higher initial installation cost but lower running costs

**2. Electric (Dry) Systems**
- Electrical heating elements beneath the floor
- Either heating mats or heating cables
- Connected to mains electricity
- Controlled via thermostats
- Lower installation cost but higher running costs

### Key Components in Water-Based Systems

**1. Heat Source**
- Boiler (gas, oil, or electric)
- Heat pump (air or ground source)
- Solar thermal system (usually supplementary)

**2. Manifold Assembly**
- Distribution manifold (flow and return headers)
- Actuators for zone control
- Flow gauges and balancing valves
- Air vents
- Temperature gauges
- Isolation valves

**3. Circulation System**
- Circulation pump
- Mixing valve (to regulate temperature)
- Expansion vessel
- Pressure relief valve

**4. Control System**
- Room thermostats (wired or wireless)
- Wiring center/controller
- Actuators
- System programmer/timer

**5. Pipework**
- PEX, PE-RT, or multi-layer composite
- Typically 16mm or 20mm diameter
- Laid in loops under the floor

### Key Components in Electric Systems

**1. Heating Elements**
- Heating mats (fixed size sheets)
- Loose cables (flexible installation)
- Heating films (usually for laminate/wood floors)

**2. Control System**
- Thermostats (often with floor sensors)
- Relay boxes for multiple zones
- Timer/programmer functions

**3. Power Supply**
- Dedicated electrical circuit
- RCD protection
- Junction boxes

## PART 2: COMMON FAULTS IN WATER-BASED UNDERFLOOR HEATING

### Single Zone Not Heating

**Symptoms:**
- One room or area remains cold
- Other zones heating correctly
- Inconsistent temperature in affected zone

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Actuator Issues**
   - Stuck actuator pin
   - Failed actuator
   - Wiring fault to actuator

2. **Air in System**
   - Airlock in specific loop
   - Inadequate bleeding
   - Air entering system

3. **Flow Restrictions**
   - Flow gauge not opened correctly
   - Debris blockage in specific loop
   - Kinked or damaged pipe

4. **Control Issues**
   - Faulty thermostat
   - Incorrectly wired zone
   - Thermostat calibration error

### Multiple Zones or Entire System Not Heating

**Symptoms:**
- All or most zones remain cold
- System appears powered but no heat output
- Pump running but no heat distribution

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Circulation Problems**
   - Pump failure
   - Seized mixing valve
   - Closed isolation valves

2. **Control System Faults**
   - Wiring center failure
   - No power to system
   - Controller programming error

3. **Heat Source Issues**
   - Boiler not receiving call for heat
   - Heat pump fault
   - Interlock preventing operation

4. **System Pressure Issues**
   - Low pressure causing boiler lockout
   - Air in main system
   - Water leak

### Temperature Control Problems

**Symptoms:**
- Overheating
- Underheating
- Temperature fluctuations

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Mixing Valve Issues**
   - Incorrect temperature setting
   - Seized or sticking valve
   - Actuator fault on valve

2. **Control System Problems**
   - Thermostat placement issues
   - Faulty temperature sensors
   - Incorrect settings

3. **Flow Rate Issues**
   - Incorrect system balancing
   - Pump speed too high/low
   - Flow restrictions

### Noise Issues

**Symptoms:**
- Gurgling sounds
- Pump noise
- Clicking from manifold

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Air in System**
   - Inadequate bleeding
   - Continuous air ingress
   - Air trapped in pump

2. **Pump Issues**
   - Pump speed too high
   - Worn pump bearings
   - Cavitation

3. **Pressure Issues**
   - Excessive system pressure
   - Expansion vessel failure
   - Pressure fluctuations

### Pressure Problems

**Symptoms:**
- Frequent pressure drops
- Excessive pressure
- Pressure relief valve activation

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Leaks**
   - Pipe connections
   - Manifold seals
   - Underfloor pipework

2. **Expansion Vessel Issues**
   - Failed diaphragm
   - Incorrect pre-charge
   - Undersized vessel

3. **Filling Issues**
   - Automatic filler faults
   - Manual filling errors
   - System oversized

## PART 3: COMMON FAULTS IN ELECTRIC UNDERFLOOR HEATING

### No Heat Output

**Symptoms:**
- Floor remains cold
- No response from system
- Thermostat appears operational

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Power Supply Issues**
   - Tripped RCD/circuit breaker
   - Failed fuse
   - Loose connections

2. **Heating Element Problems**
   - Broken element
   - Disconnected wire
   - Manufacturing fault

3. **Control System Faults**
   - Faulty thermostat
   - Floor sensor issue
   - Relay failure

### Partial Heating

**Symptoms:**
- Cold spots on floor
   - Some areas heat while others remain cold
   - Inconsistent heating across floor

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Heating Element Damage**
   - Partial break in element
   - Installation damage
   - Mat/cable separation

2. **Insulation Issues**
   - Inadequate insulation in specific areas
   - Thermal bridging
   - Heat sink effect from objects

3. **Control Issues**
   - Sensor placement problems
   - Multiple zone control fault
   - Power output limitations

### Overheating

**Symptoms:**
- Floor excessively hot
- System doesn't regulate temperature
- Thermal cutout activation

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Thermostat Issues**
   - Faulty floor sensor
   - Incorrect settings
   - Failed thermostat

2. **Installation Problems**
   - Heating elements too close together
   - Excessive insulation trapping heat
   - Incorrect element for floor type

### Thermostat Problems

**Symptoms:**
- Blank display
- Error codes
- Unresponsive controls

**Possible Causes:**

1. **Power Issues**
   - Battery failure (if battery-powered)
   - Loose connections
   - Power surge damage

2. **Sensor Problems**
   - Floor sensor failure
   - Sensor wire damage
   - Incorrect sensor type

3. **Control Unit Failure**
   - Electronic component failure
   - Software glitch
   - Water damage

## PART 4: DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURES FOR WATER-BASED UFH

### Initial Assessment

1. **System Overview Check**
   - Verify heat source is operational
   - Check programmer/timer settings
   - Confirm power to controls
   - Verify system pressure (typically 1-2 bar)

2. **Zone-by-Zone Assessment**
   - Identify which zones are affected
   - Check thermostat settings for each zone
   - Listen for actuator operation
   - Feel manifold pipes for temperature difference

3. **Manifold Inspection**
   - Check flow gauges are open (typically 1-3 turns)
   - Verify isolation valves are open
   - Look for signs of leaks
   - Check temperature gauges (supply typically 35-45°C)

### Actuator Testing

**Equipment Needed:**
- Multimeter
- Small screwdriver
- Silicone spray (for stuck pins)

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Visual Inspection**
   - Check for LED indicators on actuators
   - Look for visible damage
   - Verify proper mounting

2. **Pin Check**
   - Remove actuator (usually twist or unclip)
   - Check pin beneath actuator moves freely
   - Use silicone spray if stuck
   - Manually depress and release to test

3. **Electrical Testing**
   - Measure voltage at actuator terminals (typically 230V or 24V)
   - Check resistance of actuator coil (if applicable)
   - Verify signal from thermostat reaching actuator

### Air Lock Diagnosis and Bleeding

**Equipment Needed:**
- Allen key or radiator key for air vents
   - Container to catch water
   - Towels
   - Hoses for power flushing

**Testing and Correction Procedure:**

1. **Identify Air Presence**
   - Listen for gurgling sounds
   - Check for temperature inconsistency
   - Note cold spots in floor

2. **Manual Bleeding**
   - Turn off system
   - Open air vent on manifold slowly
   - Allow air to escape until water appears
   - Close vent and repeat on return manifold

3. **Power Flushing Single Zone**
   - Close all loops except affected one
   - Remove actuator from affected loop
   - Connect hosepipe to flow valve
   - Connect drain hose to return valve
   - Flush with mains pressure until clear

### Pump and Circulation Testing

**Equipment Needed:**
- Pressure gauge
- Screwdriver
- Multimeter

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Pump Operation Check**
   - Verify pump is running (vibration/noise)
   - Check pump settings (speed/mode)
   - Look for error lights on pump

2. **Flow Check**
   - Feel temperature difference between flow and return pipes
   - Check flow temperature at manifold
   - Verify adequate flow rate at gauges

3. **Electrical Testing**
   - Verify power supply to pump (typically 230V)
   - Check pump relay operation
   - Test controller output to pump

### Mixing Valve Diagnosis

**Equipment Needed:**
- Thermometer
- Silicone spray
- Allen keys

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Temperature Check**
   - Measure flow temperature at manifold
   - Compare with mixing valve setting
   - Verify return temperature

2. **Actuator Function**
   - Remove actuator head
   - Check pin movement
   - Verify thermostat control
   - Test manual operation

3. **Valve Body Inspection**
   - Check for scale buildup
   - Verify free movement
   - Inspect for leaks

## PART 5: DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURES FOR ELECTRIC UFH

### Initial Assessment

1. **System Overview Check**
   - Verify power supply (circuit breaker)
   - Check programmer/timer settings
   - Confirm thermostat display working
   - Test manual override function

2. **Floor Temperature Assessment**
   - Use thermal imaging camera if available
   - Check for cold spots
   - Measure floor temperature at different points
   - Compare with thermostat setting

### Electrical Testing

**Equipment Needed:**
- Multimeter
- Insulation resistance tester
- Circuit breaker finder

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Power Supply Check**
   - Verify voltage at thermostat (230V)
   - Check fuse/circuit breaker
   - Test RCD operation

2. **Heating Element Continuity Test**
   - Disconnect from thermostat
   - Measure resistance of heating element
   - Compare with manufacturer specifications
   - Check for infinite resistance (indicating break)

3. **Insulation Resistance Test**
   - Disconnect from power
   - Test between conductors and earth
   - Should be >1MΩ at 500V DC
   - Lower values indicate insulation damage

### Thermostat and Sensor Testing

**Equipment Needed:**
- Multimeter
- Manufacturer's resistance charts
- Small screwdriver

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Thermostat Power Check**
   - Verify supply voltage
   - Check display operation
   - Test button functionality

2. **Floor Sensor Testing**
   - Disconnect sensor from thermostat
   - Measure resistance of sensor
   - Compare with temperature/resistance chart
   - Check for infinite resistance (indicating break)

3. **Thermostat Output Testing**
   - Measure voltage at output terminals when calling for heat
   - Check relay operation (audible click)
   - Verify load switching capacity

### Heating Mat/Cable Fault Location

**Equipment Needed:**
- TDR (Time Domain Reflectometer) or specialized cable fault finder
- Thermal imaging camera (if available)
- Resistance meter

**Testing Procedure:**

1. **Resistance Mapping**
   - Take multiple resistance readings
   - Compare with installation diagrams
   - Identify potential break locations

2. **Thermal Imaging**
   - Power system at low voltage
   - Use thermal camera to identify cold spots
   - Map against floor plan

3. **Advanced Fault Finding**
   - Use TDR to locate cable breaks
   - Measure distance to fault
   - Mark location for repair access

## PART 6: REMEDIAL ACTIONS FOR WATER-BASED UFH

### Resolving Air Lock Issues

1. **Standard Bleeding Procedure**
   - Turn system off
   - Open air vents on manifold sequentially
   - Close when water appears
   - Check system pressure after bleeding
   - Top up if necessary

2. **Power Flushing Individual Loops**
   - Isolate affected loop
   - Connect flushing hoses
   - Flush with fresh water until clear
   - Reconnect and test

3. **Full System Power Flush**
   - Connect professional flushing machine
   - Add appropriate cleaning chemicals
   - Flush entire system
   - Refill with treated water

### Actuator Replacement

1. **Removal Procedure**
   - Turn off system
   - Remove actuator (usually twist or unclip)
   - Clean valve pin
   - Check pin operation manually

2. **Replacement Installation**
   - Ensure pin is in correct position
   - Align new actuator
   - Secure in place
   - Connect wiring
   - Test operation

3. **System Testing**
   - Power up system
   - Set thermostat to call for heat
   - Verify actuator operation
   - Check heating response

### Pump Maintenance and Replacement

1. **Basic Maintenance**
   - Check for debris in pump housing
   - Unblock if necessary
   - Set correct speed
   - Bleed air from pump

2. **Replacement Procedure**
   - Turn off system
   - Isolate pump (close valves)
   - Disconnect electrical supply
   - Remove old pump
   - Install new pump with correct orientation
   - Reconnect electrical supply
   - Open isolation valves
   - Test operation

### Fixing Control System Issues

1. **Thermostat Replacement**
   - Turn off power
   - Remove old thermostat
   - Label wires if not labeled
   - Connect new thermostat
   - Program settings
   - Test operation

2. **Wiring Center Repair**
   - Turn off power
   - Identify faulty components
   - Replace fuses/relays as needed
   - Check all connections
   - Test operation of each zone

3. **System Reprogramming**
   - Reset controller to defaults
   - Reprogram time schedules
   - Set correct temperatures
   - Test all functions

## PART 7: REMEDIAL ACTIONS FOR ELECTRIC UFH

### Repairing Damaged Heating Elements

**Note: Complete cable replacement is often more practical than repair for in-screed installations**

1. **Localized Repair (if accessible)**
   - Expose damaged section
   - Cut out damaged portion
   - Splice in replacement section using manufacturer-approved connectors
   - Reapply insulation
   - Test thoroughly before reinstating floor covering

2. **Bypass Strategy (for multiple breaks)**
   - Locate multiple breaks
   - Assess if section can be bypassed
   - Use approved splice kits
   - Insulate repairs thoroughly
   - Verify system resistance

### Thermostat Replacement

1. **Removal Procedure**
   - Turn off power
   - Remove old thermostat
   - Label wires carefully
   - Remove floor sensor if accessible

2. **Installation Procedure**
   - Install new floor sensor if possible
   - Connect wiring according to diagram
   - Mount new thermostat
   - Program settings
   - Test operation at low temperature first

3. **System Testing**
   - Monitor initial heating cycle
   - Check floor temperature response
   - Verify limiting functions
   - Test timer operation

### Resolving Electrical Supply Issues

1. **Circuit Protection Reset/Replacement**
   - Reset tripped breakers
   - Replace blown fuses
   - Check RCD operation
   - Test circuit isolation

2. **Connection Repair**
   - Identify loose connections
   - Tighten terminal screws
   - Replace damaged terminals
   - Verify secure connections

3. **Load Assessment**
   - Calculate total system load
   - Verify circuit capacity is adequate
   - Distribute load if necessary
   - Upgrade circuit protection if required

## PART 8: PREVENTATIVE MAINTENANCE

### Water-Based UFH Maintenance

**Annual Checks:**
1. **Pressure Verification**
   - Check system pressure (1-2 bar cold)
   - Top up if necessary
   - Look for pressure drops indicating leaks

2. **Visual Inspection**
   - Check manifold for leaks
   - Inspect pump for signs of failure
   - Look for moisture around fittings
   - Verify actuator operation

3. **Performance Assessment**
   - Check flow temperatures (typically 35-45°C)
   - Verify return temperatures
   - Test response time to thermostat changes
   - Check heat distribution across floors

4. **System Protection**
   - Check inhibitor levels
   - Add inhibitor if necessary
   - Clean filters
   - Check expansion vessel pressure

### Electric UFH Maintenance

**Annual Checks:**
1. **Electrical Inspection**
   - Test RCD operation
   - Check thermostat function
   - Verify timing programs
   - Test manual override

2. **Performance Assessment**
   - Measure floor warm-up time
   - Check temperature accuracy
   - Verify even heat distribution
   - Test energy consumption if metered

3. **Floor Sensor Verification**
   - Check sensor readings
   - Compare with actual temperatures
   - Test sensor resistance if accessible

### Water Temperature Guide for Different Floor Coverings

| Floor Covering | Maximum Flow Temperature | Notes |
|----------------|--------------------------|-------|
| Tile/Stone | 45°C | High thermal conductivity |
| Engineered Wood | 40°C | Check manufacturer limits |
| Vinyl/Linoleum | 35°C | Risk of damage above 27°C floor temp |
| Carpet | 40-45°C | Depends on thickness/tog value |
| Solid Wood | 35°C | Gradual warm-up essential |

### Normal Operating Values (Water-Based UFH)

| Parameter | Normal Range | Warning Signs |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| Flow Temperature | 35-45°C | >50°C or <30°C |
| Return Temperature | 25-35°C | <5°C differential from flow |
| System Pressure (cold) | 1.0-1.5 bar | <0.8 bar or >2.5 bar |
| Pump Head | 3-6m | Inadequate for design |
| Flow Rate | 1.5-2.5 L/min per loop | <1.0 L/min indicates restriction |

## PART 10: SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

### Working with Water-Based Systems

1. **Pressure Hazards**
   - Never open system under pressure
   - Use pressure gauge before work
   - Allow system to cool before work
   - Be aware of scalding risk from hot water

2. **Electrical Safety**
   - Isolate power before working on pumps/controls
   - Verify isolation with test equipment
   - Use RCD protection
   - Keep electrical components away from water

3. **Chemical Safety**
   - Wear gloves when handling inhibitors
   - Follow manufacturer's dilution instructions
   - Avoid skin contact with system water
   - Provide adequate ventilation

### Working with Electric Systems

1. **Electrical Hazards**
   - Always disconnect power before work
   - Verify isolation with test equipment
   - Use RCD protection
   - Never modify heating elements
   - Work within competence limits

2. **Testing Safety**
   - Use insulated tools
   - Never touch live parts
   - High resistance tests should only be performed by qualified persons
   - Follow manufacturer's test procedures

3. **Repair Limitations**
   - Know when replacement is safer than repair
   - Never compromise on connection quality
   - Use only manufacturer-approved parts
   - Document all changes made
